Getting started
with tchop™.

Understanding channels,
mixes and cards.

Content in tchop™ follows a simple structure of channels and mixes, which is easy to grasp if you think of content on your smartphone.

Mobile content is organized in thematic feeds in chronological order. News feeds can be a mix of all kinds of relevant content, like short posts, images, tweets or videos.

We at tchop™ belief that smart content curation is the key to maximize simplicity and efficiency for the ones providing such news feeds to their employees, clients, stakeholders or team mates.

Create thefirst channelwithin yourorganisation

When you sign up we will ask you to set up the name of your first channel as well as the name of your organization.

A channel is the core of your tchop™ content strategy and the place to get started. It can hold multiple news feeds with it´s own group of editors and users.

An organization can hold multiple channels, that aim at different target groups like departments, regions or topics. A flexible structure that works both for small teams as well as big enterprises.

Add mixesto your channel

Your channel dashboard is your main control panel. There you can add mixes to your channel as thematic feeds of content. Mixes can be internal news, external press clippings, news from HR department or any other compilation of content. Of course mixes can also hold static evergreen content you always want to make available. It´s all up to you.

Publish, unpublish, sort or sync mixes between channels with a few clicks. It´s incredibly easy and powerful.

Integratecontent feeds

Adding content manually can take quite some time, so we´ve build integrations that do this job for you.

Integrations can automatically add cards to any mix. They can feed in news or social feeds from any kind of source, scrape websites or connect to your slack team. You can automatically publish content and create fully automated workflows. Or you can collect, structure, approve or edit content before you distribute it.
